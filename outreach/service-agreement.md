# Simple Service Agreement (Optional but Recommended)

For anything beyond the smallest deals, sending a short written agreement
(even informally, over email/text) protects both you and the client. This
isn't a legal document — just a plain-English summary both sides agree to.

## Template — copy, fill in, and send before starting real (paid) work

```
Website Service Agreement (Informal)

Client: [Business Name]
Provider: Hasweeni Digital

Scope of work:
- One custom website with the sections shown in the demo
  ([DEMO LINK])
- Mobile-friendly design
- Hosting setup (client pays nothing extra for hosting)
- [Any extras agreed to, e.g. "custom domain setup assistance"]

Price: $[PRICE], due before final publishing

Timeline: Site will be finalized and published within [X] business days of
payment being received

Revisions: Up to [2] rounds of reasonable text/photo changes included after
the demo. Larger changes (new pages, major redesigns) may be quoted
separately.

Ownership: Once paid in full, the website content and design are the
client's to use. Hosting remains on GitHub Pages unless the client requests
migration to their own hosting/domain (client's responsibility to set up
and pay for, if desired).

Both parties agree to the above.
```

## Why bother with this for small jobs?
For your very first few $150-250 demos, a full agreement can feel like
overkill — a simple back-and-forth message confirming price and scope is
usually enough. Start using this more formally once deals cross ~$400, or
if a client asks for anything unusual (e.g. extra pages, ongoing
maintenance) — having it in writing avoids misunderstandings on both sides.
