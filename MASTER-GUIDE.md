This is the complete playbook: build websites for real US local service
businesses (starting with HVAC) that don't have one yet, using a template
that's already built for you. Everything here costs **$0** to run.

## Step 1 — Understand the model (2 min read)

You find a real US local business (HVAC company, plumber, etc.) that has no
website. You build them a free demo site (already done for you — the
template exists). You send it to them with zero pressure. If they like it,
they pay, and you make it their official live site.

**Why this works:** these business owners rely on Google searches from
customers. Not having a website costs them real business. Seeing a finished,
professional site with their own name on it is far more persuasive than any
sales pitch.

## Step 2 — Pick your first lead (0 min — already done)

Open [`leads/pampa-tx-hvac-leads.md`](leads/pampa-tx-hvac-leads.md). Start with **Pampa Heating and Air**
or **H & S Air Conditioning & Heating Systems** — both have strong ratings
and no website.

## Step 3 — Get a demo built for that business

Message the owner (via this chat) with the business's name, phone number,
and city from the lead list. The demo site will be built and published live
within minutes at a real link like:
`https://momowm.github.io/Hasweeni/<business-name>/`

## Step 4 — Find a way to contact them

1. Search Facebook for `"[Business Name]" [City] [State]` — most small
   businesses have a Facebook Page even with no website. Message them there.
2. No Facebook Page? Call the phone number directly, or look for an
   Instagram account.

## Step 5 — Send the first message

Use the exact templates in [`outreach/messages.md`](outreach/messages.md) — message #1 (Messenger/DM)
or #2 (Email). Personalize the business name and send.

## Step 6 — Follow up once if needed

If no response after 3-4 days, send follow-up message #3 from
[`outreach/messages.md`](outreach/messages.md). If still nothing, move to the next lead — don't
over-invest in one unresponsive business.

## Step 7 — Close the deal

When they respond positively, use message templates #4-6 in
[`outreach/messages.md`](outreach/messages.md) to explain pricing and next steps. Start with an
introductory price of **$150-$250** for your first few clients to build up
reviews fast — raise prices to $400-$800 after you have 2-3 happy clients.

## Step 8 — Get paid safely

Follow [`outreach/invoice-and-payment.md`](outreach/invoice-and-payment.md) exactly:
1. Send invoice once they agree
2. **Wait until payment actually lands** before removing the "DEMO PREVIEW"
   banner or treating the site as final
3. Once paid, the demo banner gets removed and the site becomes their real,
   final website

## Step 9 — Repeat

Go back to Step 2 with a new lead (or find new ones — see the
"finding more leads" section in [`leads/pampa-tx-hvac-leads.md`](leads/pampa-tx-hvac-leads.md)). Each repeat
gets faster since the template and process are already built.

## Problems You Will Run Into (and exactly what to do)

### "I sent messages but nobody's responding"
This is normal — cold outreach typically gets a 5-15% response rate even
when done well. **Fix:** message more leads in parallel (aim for 10-15 out
per week, not 1-2), and make sure the demo link actually works before
sending (test it yourself first).

### "Someone took the free demo and never paid"
This is exactly what the **DEMO PREVIEW banner** protects against — the site
is never fully "theirs" (no custom domain, watermark still visible, code not
handed over) until they pay. Never remove the banner or transfer real
ownership before payment lands.

### "A business owner wants to call me / video call me"
Nothing wrong with taking the call if you're comfortable — clear, confident
English works fine over the phone. If you'd rather not, it's completely
acceptable to say you prefer to work over text/email — many freelancers work
this way, and it's usually seen as normal rather than suspicious.

### "There's a big time difference between Iraq and the US"
Iraq is roughly 7-10 hours ahead of US time zones. **Fix:** rely on
async channels (email, Facebook Messenger, text) rather than needing live
calls — business owners are used to messaging on their own schedule anyway.
If a call is truly needed, aim for messaging them in the evening Iraq time,
which lines up with US morning hours.

### "They're asking why I'm overseas / seem hesitant because of that"
Be honest if asked directly, but you don't need to lead with it. Focus the
conversation on the finished product and fair pricing — the demo site
speaks for itself regardless of where it was built.

### "I don't have any reviews/testimonials yet"
Everyone starts at zero. Price your first 2-3 projects lower specifically to
earn real testimonials fast, then quote those testimonials to future
prospects (with permission) and raise prices.

### "A competitor agency is already contacting the same businesses"
You have two real advantages: (1) you lead with a finished free demo instead
of a cold pitch, which most agencies don't bother doing, and (2) you can
usually turn work around faster since the base template already exists.

### "The business wants extra features the template doesn't have"
It's fine to say "let me look into that" and come back — most requests
(extra pages, a photo gallery, online booking) can be added. If it's a big
ask, that's a fair reason to price higher than your standard rate.

### "How does the money actually get from the US client to Hasweeni in Iraq?"
The client pays into the account owner's US bank/Zelle/Stripe (see
[`outreach/invoice-and-payment.md`](outreach/invoice-and-payment.md)). The owner then wires or otherwise sends
Hasweeni his share directly — this has already been arranged.

### "I'm worried about scaling this / it's a lot of manual work per client"
The template + process here is built so most of the "building" work is
already handled. Your main time investment is finding leads and messaging
them — the part no template can replace. As you go, keep a running list of
what parts of outreach take the longest and that's the first thing to
streamline further.

### "The GitHub Pages link doesn't look like a 'real' business domain"
That's true and fine for the demo/early stage — `github.io` links are 100%
functional, free, and completely normal for freelancers to use early on. If
a client wants a custom domain (e.g. `pampaheatingandair.com`), that's a
~$10-15/year cost the *client* pays for (not Hasweeni), often positioned as
an optional upgrade once they're already sold on the site itself.
